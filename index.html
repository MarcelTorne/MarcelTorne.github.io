I am a Master's student in Computational Science and Engineering at Harvard University doing my thesis in Reinforcement Learning and Robotics at MIT under the supervision of Professor Pulkit Agrawal and Professor Abhishek Gupta. My interest lies in building assistive robots to help the elderly in their daily life. To get there, we should advance the field in generalizable and transferable robotic controllers — one of my ideas is by using foundation models from computer vision and natural language processing,

I received my Bachelor's degree from École Polytechnique Fédérale de Lausanne (EPFL), where I was lucky enough to do my bachelor's project at MLO and iGH under the supervision of Professor Martin Jaggi. My research was on decentralized ML and CV. I launched an open-source platform, DISCO, that allows a wide variety of users, from researchers to healthcare practitioners, to train ML models in a decentralized fashion

I am fortunate enough to be supported by the Real Colegio Complutense scholarship a merit-based scholarship from Harvard. I spend my free time I exercising — sailing, running, and hiking; or reading books — my latest reads are Notes from Underground and American Gods.
