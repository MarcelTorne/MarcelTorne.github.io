Marcel Torne
Villasevil

PhD Student · Stanford University

My dream is to design the next generation of learning-based assistive robots. I study methods for scalable data collection and rapid policy adaptation to unseen scenarios, with an emphasis on human-centric approaches.

Previously, I completed my Master's at Harvard University supported by the Real Colegio Complutense scholarship, and was part of the Improbable AI Lab at MIT CSAIL under Pulkit Agrawal and Abhishek Gupta. Before that, I received my Bachelor's from EPFL, where I built DisCO — an open-source platform for federated and decentralized ML training.

In industry I have worked at Microsoft Bing and Amazon Search on ranking algorithms.

Outside research I run, sail, and bike; play the baroque guitar; and read.
